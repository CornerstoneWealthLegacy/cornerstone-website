What Probate Really Costs

Beyond the dollar figure, probate imposes costs your family can't get back.

📰
Public Record — Total Exposure

Your assets, debts, and beneficiaries become public record. Anyone — creditors, estranged relatives, bad actors — can read the file at the courthouse.

⏳
Family Waits Months or Years

Heirs cannot receive a dollar until the court closes the estate. Mortgages, bills, and living expenses continue — but the estate is frozen.

👨‍⚖️
A Judge Controls Distribution

Every distribution requires court approval. Any beneficiary can object, triggering expensive hearings. Family disputes become public litigation.

🏠
Real Estate Frozen in Place

You cannot sell, refinance, or transfer real property during probate without a court order. If the market shifts, your heirs absorb the loss.

✈️
Out-of-State Property = Two Probates

Every state where you own real property requires its own ancillary probate. Two states means two courts, two attorneys, double the cost and time.

💔
Grief Meets Bureaucracy

Surviving spouses and children spend 18 months navigating court filings, creditor notices, and hearings — while grieving. The emotional toll is immeasurable.
