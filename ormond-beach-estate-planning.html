Estate Planning for Ormond Beach Residents

A sound Florida plan typically combines a will, a revocable living trust, a durable power of attorney, a health care surrogate, and a living will. For coastal owners, the titling of real estate deserves special attention: a funded revocable trust or an enhanced life estate (Lady Bird) deed can transfer the home to heirs outside of probate while preserving your Florida homestead protections during your lifetime.

  • Wills and revocable living trusts structured around your property
  • Lady Bird (enhanced life estate) deeds to pass real estate without probate
  • Homestead protection for your primary Florida residence
  • Durable power of attorney and advance directives

Snowbirds and Two-State Owners

Many Ormond Beach residents divide the year between Florida and another state. If Florida is your legal residence, your plan should be governed by Florida law and your homestead claimed here. Owning real property in more than one state can force your family into a second, "ancillary" probate after death — a result that planning ahead with a trust or proper deed can usually prevent.

Probate in Volusia County

For Ormond Beach residents, estate administration takes place in Volusia County, part of Florida's Seventh Judicial Circuit. Probate is filed with the Volusia County Clerk of the Circuit Court; the main courthouse is in DeLand, with a branch in Daytona Beach. Florida uses electronic court filing, so personal representatives seldom need to appear in person — but statutory deadlines, including the creditor notice period, still apply.

Ormond Beach Estate Planning FAQs

How does a waterfront or second home affect my estate plan?

Real property is a leading reason Florida estates go through probate. Titling an Ormond Beach home through a revocable living trust or a Lady Bird deed can transfer it to heirs without a Volusia County probate while keeping your homestead protections in place.

I split time between Ormond Beach and another state — where should my plan be based?

If Florida is your legal residence, your plan should be built under Florida law and your homestead claimed here. Property in two states can trigger ancillary probate; a trust or proper deed can avoid that second proceeding.

Can I complete my Ormond Beach estate plan remotely?

Yes. We serve Ormond Beach clients by phone and video, prepare documents remotely, and guide you through signing under Florida's requirements. In-person appointments are available when preferred.

Where is probate handled for Ormond Beach residents?

Ormond Beach is in Volusia County (Seventh Judicial Circuit). Probate is filed with the Volusia County Clerk; the main courthouse is in DeLand with a Daytona Beach branch, and most filings are electronic.
